📸 Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

Name: Xiangru Huang

🤳 Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

📝 Description & Explanation:

📷 As shown below, the left image was taken from a distance of around 200 cm using a focal length of 84mm, while the right image was captured from a much closer distance of around 20 cm using a focal length of 14mm. The framing was adjusted so that the size of the face appears roughly the same in both images.

🔍 Although the face size remains similar in both images, there are two significant visual differences caused by the change in focal length and camera distance:

1. Facial distortion:

✅ In the left image (84mm, 200cm), the facial proportions look natural and undistorted.
❌ In the right image (14mm, 20cm), the face appears distorted: the nose looks larger, while the ears appear stretched.

2. Background appearance:

⛱️ In the left image (84mm, zoomed in), the background elements appear closer together. In the right image (14mm, no zoom), the background appears much wider and more spaced out.

Explanation:

Shooting from a close distance with a shorter focal length exaggerates perspective, causing distortion. Shooting from farther away with a longer focal length compresses perspective, making proportions natural and background closer.

🏛️ Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

📝 Description & Explanation:

🏞️ The left image was taken using a focal length of 51mm from a farther distance. The right image was taken closer, with a focal length of 26mm.

1. Size of the distant trees: 🌳 Longer focal length compresses depth → trees look larger.

2. Number of visible columns: 🏛️ Longer focal length shows more columns compared to wide-angle.
